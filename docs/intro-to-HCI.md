# Initial Design Assignment (Due May 22)

## Overview

This first assignment gives you a high-level introduction to the kind of thinking we will practise throughout CPSC 344. You will work through a simplified version of the [Double Diamond design process](https://www.fluxspace.io/resources/the-4-ds-double-diamond-design-thinking-model) by identifying a need, refining it into a clearer design problem, brainstorming possible solutions, and choosing one approach based on design goals.

The goal of this assignment is not to create a perfect product idea. Instead, it is meant to help you get a first sense of the course material and the kinds of questions we will return to throughout the semester: What do people need? How do we frame design problems? How do we decide what makes one design better than another? What trade-offs do designers need to consider?

This is an introductory assignment, so you are not expected to have prior experience with design, HCI, or the Double Diamond model.

---

## Assignment Objectives

By completing this assignment, you will:

- Practise identifying a human need or opportunity for design.
- Learn how an initial idea can be refined into a clearer problem statement.
- Develop early experience creating design goals.
- Brainstorm multiple possible approaches before choosing one.
- Practise explaining and justifying a design decision.
- Get an initial preview of the type of design thinking used in this course.

---

## What You Will Do

In this assignment, you will move through four stages:

1. **Explore Opportunities**  
   You will begin with something in your life that you would like a design to help with. You will write an initial need statement, reflect on how it could be improved, and revise it.

2. **Define the Problem**  
   You will identify possible design considerations and choose three design goals that matter most for your problem.

3. **Brainstorm Approaches**  
   You will generate five different possible solutions. These ideas do not need to be perfect, original, or technically feasible. The goal is to explore different directions.

4. **Choose an Approach**  
   You will compare your possible solutions against your design goals, choose one preferred direction, and explain your reasoning.

## What We Are Looking For

A strong submission will show clear and thoughtful engagement with the design process. You do not need to produce a perfect solution. Instead, focus on showing how your thinking develops across the assignment.

In particular, your submission should show that you can:

- Start from a human need or opportunity.
- Refine an initial idea into a clearer need statement.
- Identify meaningful design goals.
- Generate multiple possible solutions.
- Compare design options using your goals.
- Explain your final choice clearly and thoughtfully.
