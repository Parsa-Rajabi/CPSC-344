# Extra Resources

This page collects optional resources for students who want more examples, practice, or background beyond lectures and workshops. These resources are not required readings unless a milestone, lecture, or workshop explicitly says so.

Use this page when you want to:

- review a course concept from another explanation
- find examples of HCI research, design process, or evaluation methods
- explore tools for prototyping or presenting design work
- look for optional study support

> [!NOTE]
> External resources can be useful, but they do not replace the course instructions, rubrics, ethics requirements, or submission rules. If a resource conflicts with CPSC 344 instructions, follow the course page or ask the teaching team.

## HCI and Design Foundations

Use these when you want broader context for user-centred design, everyday interaction, or design thinking.

- [The Design of Everyday Things by Don Norman](https://ebookcentral.proquest.com/lib/ubc/detail.action?docID=1167019)
- [Revamped Double Diamond: How to apply a design thinking, HCD, UX or any creative process from scratch](https://medium.com/user-experience-design-1/how-to-solve-problems-applying-a-uxdesign-designthinking-hcd-or-any-design-process-from-scratch-v2-aa16e2dd550b)
