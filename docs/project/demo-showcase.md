# Project Demo Showcase

> [!NOTE]
> Showcase logistics may be updated closer to the showcase date. Please check this page and course Slack for the latest information.

## Overview

The Project Demo Showcase is the public-facing presentation of your final prototype.

> [!WARNING]
> Each student must complete and submit the [TCPS Ethics Certification](project/ethics-certification.md) before participating in any course project work.

During the showcase, your team will demonstrate your prototype, explain your design process, and answer questions from visitors, peers, TAs, and the teaching team.

Visitors should be able to understand:

- what problem your team addressed
- who the design is for
- what your prototype does
- how the prototype supports your design goals
- what your team learned through evaluation

## Attendance and Participation

All group members are expected to attend and participate in person.

Absence tokens cannot be used for the final showcase. Refer to the syllabus for the workshop attendance and participation policy.

Each team member should be prepared to explain the project, demonstrate the prototype, and answer questions.

For help assigning demo roles, planning backups, and making sure every team member is prepared, see the [Group Work Resources](group-work-resources.md).

## Showcase Format

Each team will run a demo station.

Your demo may be:

- a guided scripted demo
- a semi-guided walkthrough
- a hands-on interactive demo
- a combination of presentation and visitor interaction

Choose the format that best helps visitors understand and experience your prototype.

## Demo Preparation

Come prepared with:

- a working prototype
- any device, adapter, charger, or accessory needed to run it
- a short demo script or plan
- a backup plan in case of technical issues
- a short visual summary of your project

Your visual summary should include:

- need statement
- central tasks
- design requirements
- chosen design approach
- prototype usage instructions, if needed

## Showcase Materials

Your team should prepare a Demo Materials folder or equivalent collection of links.

This may include:

- prototype files or prototype link
- README or usage instructions
- demo script
- visual summary
- setup instructions
