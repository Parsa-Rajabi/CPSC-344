# Course Project

> [!NOTE]
> This project page is still in development and subject to change as milestone descriptions, rubrics, and templates are finalized. Please check back regularly for updates.

## Overview

The course project is a semester-long group project where your team will apply HCI methods to investigate a human need, design problem, or design opportunity, then develop and evaluate a prototype.

Across the project, your team will move through the following stages:

1. Identify and pitch a problem space.
2. Investigate user needs and define design requirements.
3. Explore multiple design approaches.
4. Plan and build a prototype.
5. Plan and conduct an evaluation.
6. Present the final prototype and report on your design process.

The project is designed to help you practice the full arc of HCI work, from early problem framing to evidence-based design recommendations.

## Design Decision Logs

Beginning with Project Milestone 2, each project deliverable includes a short **Design Decision Log**. The purpose of the log is to help your team explain how the project changed over time and why those changes were reasonable design decisions.

Use the log to record substantive changes to your project direction, scope, stakeholders, research questions, design requirements, prototype, evaluation plan, or final recommendations. You do not need to record every small edit.

Each log entry should answer four questions:

| Decision | What changed? | Why did it change? | Evidence or feedback used |
| :------: | :-----------: | :----------------: | :-----------------------: |
| We narrowed our user group. | From all commuters to first-year students commuting to campus. | The original scope was too broad for the course timeline. | TA feedback and interview access constraints. |

Strong logs connect revisions to evidence, feedback, feasibility, or a clearer understanding of the problem. If your team does not accept an important TA suggestion, you must include that decision in the log and explain why.

## Attendance and Participation Reminder

The project depends on active in-person participation. Workshops are used for collaboration, feedback, project progress, and presentation activities.

Some project sessions, including Shark Tank, demo presentations, peer testing sessions, and the final showcase, require all group members to attend in person. Refer to the syllabus for the full [Workshop Attendance & Participation](syllabus.md#workshop-attendance--participation) policy.

## Peer Evaluation Reminder

Several project components include peer evaluation. Peer evaluation is used to help account for individual contribution within group-based work.

When a project milestone includes peer evaluation, each team member will evaluate their teammates’ contributions using a structured form. Refer to the syllabus section on [Group Peer Evaluation](syllabus.md#group-peer-evaluation), the [group work resources](group-work-resources.md), and the relevant milestone page for details.

## Final Reminder

The project is cumulative. Each stage should build on the previous one.

A strong project shows a clear chain of reasoning:

> Human need → research questions → need-finding study → design requirements → design approaches → prototype → evaluation → findings → recommendations

Keep your project materials organized as you go. Earlier sketches, notes, feedback, study materials, and prototype versions may be useful later in the report, appendix, demo, or team reflection.
