## Overview

In this milestone, your team will submit your final prototype and a final design report.

> [!WARNING]
> Each student must complete and submit the [TCPS Ethics Certification](project/ethics-certification.md) before participating in any course project work.

Your report should explain how your design developed over time, how you evaluated it, what you learned, and what you would recommend next. Your prototype should demonstrate the main interaction your team designed and support the central tasks you identified earlier in the project.

The goal is not to claim that your design is perfect. The goal is to show a clear, evidence-based design process:

> Need statement → design requirements → design approach → prototype → evaluation → findings → recommendations

## Learning Objectives

After completing this milestone, you should be able to:

- Develop a medium to high fidelity prototype that supports your central tasks.
- Evaluate your prototype using appropriate HCI methods.
- Analyze qualitative and/or quantitative evaluation data.
- Explain how your design changed based on feedback, prototyping, and evaluation.
- Connect major design revisions to evidence, feedback, feasibility, and design reasoning.
- Communicate design outcomes through a clear written report and live demo.
- Reflect critically on your design process and identify future improvements.

## Formatting and Page Limits

Use the following formatting for the final report:

- **Font size:** 12 pt minimum for body text
- **Font:** readable standard font, such as Arial, Calibri, Times New Roman, or equivalent
- **Margins:** 1 inch on all sides
- **Spacing:** single-spaced or 1.15-spaced
- **Captions and tables:** 9 pt minimum
- **Visuals:** clear and legible when printed at 8.5 x 11 inches. Add annotations or labels as needed to ensure clarity when printed at this size.
- **File format:** PDF, unless otherwise stated by the teaching team

Do not shrink font sizes, margins, spacing, visuals, or tables to fit more content into the page limit.

### Page Limit

The main report body must be **maximum 7 pages**.

The following do **not** count toward the 7-page main body limit:

- cover page
- table of contents, if included
- design decision log
- appendix

## Prototype Expectations

Your prototype should be medium to high fidelity.

It does not need to be a fully finished product, but it should be complete enough to support your central tasks, evaluation goals, and demo.

Your prototype submission should make clear:

- what users can do
- how users move through the main interaction
- what parts are functional
- what parts are simulated or simplified
- what parts are outside the current scope

If your prototype requires setup, include a short README with:

<details>
<summary><strong>README checklist</strong></summary>

- prototype link or file location
- required software, account, or device
- setup instructions
- known limitations
- what parts are functional or simulated

</details>

Make sure all links are accessible to the teaching team.

<summary><strong>Exceeds Expectations</strong></summary>

**1. Design Decision Log (5 points)**  
The log contains 4–6 concise entries that clearly tie design changes to specific evidence (e.g., TA feedback, study data, evaluation results). Each entry follows the required format and effectively traces the project’s evolution. If a TA suggestion was not adopted, the entry includes a clear justification for that decision. In cases where no changes were made, at least one entry provides a detailed discussion of the considerations that supported maintaining the current direction.

**2. Introduction (5 points)**  
The introduction well defines the problem and design approach, making it accessible to readers without any project background. The need statement and design goals are clearly and concisely summarized, and the chosen approach is directly justified in relation to both.

**3. Conceptual Model Iterations (18-20 points)**  
The section details the initial model and key iterative changes, with explicit integration of feedback. All relevant objects, actions, and relationships are fully described. Readers can understand the user's step-by-step process, including user expectations and system responses after each action. It presents effective visuals and compelling reasoning, all clearly tied to the findings. Improvements to the conceptual model are well justified, and all decisions are supported by evidence from evaluation and iteration.

**4. Prototype Methods and Evaluation (18-20 points)**  
The prototyping approach and plan are well justified, and the prototype successfully achieves its objectives. Its fidelity, including what is functional, simulated, and polished, is thoroughly described, with clear justification for each choice. The evaluation methods are directly aligned with the design goals, and the rationale for both the evaluation and the prototype is well reasoned. The study description provides sufficient detail, particularly for summarizing the protocol, and uses in-text references effectively to direct readers to the appendix and/or the Design Decision Log when necessary. The total number of participants is more than twice the number of team members. It includes a thoughtful presentation of 3 clear research questions, supported by detailed methods, participant sample, procedure, tasks, and data collected. Justifications are strengthened by triangulating the research questions. 

**5. Analysis & Visualization (18-20 points)**  
The results section presents a clear claim, specific evidence, stated confidence, and insightful, distinct limitations for all 3 research questions. Any insufficient findings are explicitly acknowledged and explained, with at most one research question affected. Visualizations are effective, correctly referenced, and directly support the claims. Each claim is presented with full line of reasoning (from data and analysis through results, interpretation, visualization, and recommendation), so that it is unambiguous.

**6. Recommendations and Process Critique (14-15 points)**  
Design recommendations are specific, actionable, and directly tied to evaluation findings, detailed and realistic enough to inform future research and implementation. The process critique offers an insightful and thoughtful reflection on what worked, what did not, and identifies clear opportunities for improvement and change (e.g., goes beyond the obvious critiques and future work).

**7. Formatting, Clarity, & Communication (9-10 points)**  
The main report body and each section adhere to the specified page limits, with all formatting rules strictly followed. The cover page and table of contents include all required information correctly, and all figures and tables have numbered labels and captions. The writing is clear, concise, and professional, with minimal or no grammatical errors or typos. The report is fully understandable without reference to the appendix, includes an AI disclosure and team contributions, and all links are accessible.
